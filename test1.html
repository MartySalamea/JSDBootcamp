Many people believe that the rich only become rich at the expense of others. This world view is also called zero-sum thinking because its adherents regard economic life as a zero-sum game, just like tennis, where one player has to lose for the other to win. In his poem “Alfabet,” the German poet Bertolt Brecht formulated this zero-sum mindset as follows: “Said the poor man with a twitch: Were I not poor, you wouldn’t be rich.”
